My Educational Journey

Click on the above buttons to see what I learned each semester at Michigan State University. Each semester has a brief summary of the courses I took, as well as concepts I learned and projects I completed.

Tech

CSE 232

Introduction to Programming II

Introductory-level programming class in C++. Data types, the STL, generic algorithms, templates, structs, classes, dynamic memory allocation, and more. Projects included a shopping list which searches for the cheapest items and a DNA protein sequencer.

CSE 260

Discrete Structures in Computer Science

Discrete math for Computer Science applications. Begin with propositional logic and proofs, move to countability and set theory, and end with finite automata. This class taught me what functions and relations REALLY were.

CSE 320

Computer Organization and Architecture

Abstraction: the class. Start with basic digital logic and circuits on one side and C programming on the other. Build up more complex combinatorial and sequential circuits with the help of truth tables, karnaugh maps, and state machines while learning lower-level binary and data concepts with ARM Assembly. Finally, the two paths converge in a project in which I created a microprocessor which runs ARM Thumb machine code. I've been a TA for this course for two semesters.

CSE 331

Algorithms and Data Structures

Design, analysis, and implementation of fundamental algorithms and data structures. Linked lists, stacks, queues, trees, hash tables, graphs and more. Many searching algorithms including Dijkstra's algorithm, A* search, BFS, and DFS, as well as sorting algorithms like merge sort, insertion sort, selection sort, and heap sort. Analyze time and space complexity using big-O notation.

CSE 300

Social, Ethical, and Professional Issues in Computer Science

Impact of computing on society, and the professional and ethical responsibilities inherent to computing. Analysis of incidents such as the Therac-25 and discussion of topics such as intellectual property, privacy, and data.

CSE 335

Object-Oriented Software Design

Large-scale software development using C++. Object-Oriented design using UML diagrams, inheritance, and polymorphism. Build GUI applications and libraries. First major project was a space shooter built in teams of five using Git for version control and collaboration. Second major project was a library for animation software.

CSE 460

Computability and Formal Lanugage Theory

Explore what problems are solvable using basic models of computation. Mathematical induction proofs, set theory, countability, program behavior problems, the Halting Problem, and Church's Thesis. Regular languages, context-free language, deterministic and non-deterministic finite automata, and pushdown automata. I was a TA for this course in the Fall of 2022.

CSE 325

Computer Systems

Virtualization: the class. Explore standard computer systems and protocols. Processor operation and memory management. Operating system processes, kernels, and file systems. Parallelism and concurrency. Networking and network protocols. Examine security vulnerabilities of systems and solutions.

CSE 402

Biometrics and Pattern Recognition

Taught by the esteemed Dr. Arun Ross. The math, statistics, and algorithms behind biometric systems: feature extraction, matching, comparison, and scoring of fingerprints, faces, and irides. Includes many fun computer vision techniques.

CSE 422

Computer Networks

Deep dive on the architecture and protocols in each layer of the Internet Protocol Suite. Application, Transport, Network, and Data layers. Socket programming in C++; one project was an online game, another was a web proxy.

CSE 415

Introduction to Parallel Computing

Parallel and distributed models of computing. Pipelining, cache hierarchies, uniform and distributed architectures, synchronization, message passing, partitioning, Amdahl's Law, and data dependencies. OpenMP, MPI, and CUDA programming in C.

CSE 425

Introduction to Computer Security

Secure computing practices and defense against cyber attacks. CIA triad, cryptography, access control, network security, and mobile protocols. One project involved using Ettercap to launch an ARP poisining and DNS spoof attack to redirect victims to a malicious site on an insecure network. Another project was launching SQL injections against the Damn Vulnerable Web Application.

Business

ACC 230

Survey of Accounting Concepts

A brief exploration of managerial and financial accounting concepts. Cost flows, estimation, and analysis. Using accounting to make decisions such as make-or-buy or special order. Master budgets and variance analysis. This is the course that got me more interested in business.

EC 201

Introduction to Microeconomics

Economics, but at an individual level. Supply, demand, and opportunity cost in the short and long run. Effects of taxes, price controls, monopolies, and trade.

EC 202

Introduction to Macroeconomics

Economics, but at a macro level. Supply, demand, and opportunity cost. Taxes, GDP, inflation, employment, aggregate expenditures, monetary and fiscal policy, and international trade.

FI 320

Introduction to Finance

Introduction to investing: valuation, time value of money, bonds, stocks, and mutual funds. Financial literacy with income statements and balance sheets. Financial planning, capital budgeting, and decision making.

MKT 317

Market Analytics

Statisitcal analysis using R and data visualization using Tableau. Applications of analytics to decisions and effective communication of results. Models and relationships between variables. ANOVA, T-Tests, and Chi-Squared tests.

SCM 371

Procurement and Supply Management

The purchasing process and its strategic dimensions. Supplier evaluations and scorecards. Strategic sourcing matrix, category analysis, lean, e-sourcing, negotiation, and supplier integration. Presented a sourcing recommendation for the final project.

SCM 372

Manufacturing and Service Operations Management

Operations management for production of goods and services. Product desgin, demand forecasting methods, capacity planning, JIT manufacturing, process analysis and control, lean, six sigma, MRP, and master scheduling. Made capacity decisions based on forecasts in a simulation of a medical testing lab.

Engineering

MTH 234

Multivariable Calculus

Calculus, but multivariable. Vectors, functions, and planes in a three-dimensional space. Multiple integrals, line integrals, and surface integrals.

EGR 100

Introduction to Engineering Design

Interdisciplinary introduction course for engineers with a large focus on teamwork. For the major course projects, I coded a To-Do list mobile app using MIT AppInventor, calculated parameters for the design of a heat exchanger, and contributed to scientific reports. Learned about professional standards and expectations of the field.

MTH 314

Matrix Algebra with Computational Applications

Introduction to many linear algebra concepts such as transformations, Gauss-Jordan elimination, eigenvalues, eigenvectors, vector spaces, SVD, PCA, and more, using Python and NumPy.

STT 351

Probability and Statistics for Engineering

Probability, estimation, confidence intervals, hypothesis testing, and linear regression for engineering problems. Used Python libraries such as NumPy and matplotlib in the Honors section for analysis.

MTH 235

Differential Equations

Took a year-long break from calculus and returned to differential equations. Seperable, exact, and linear equations. Laplace transforms and Fourier series. Matrices and eigenvalues.

CE 221

Statics

The sum of the forces is zero and the sum of the moments is zero. Equilibrium of particles and rigid bodies. Friction, trusses, and beams.

ME 280

Graphic Communications

3D computer-adided design using Siemens NX. Sketching, modeling, constraints and assemblies. Blueprint reading and ASME standards.

AESC 210

Global Systems: Engineering, Economics, Environment

System dynamics nd modeling with MATLAB. Weekly presentations on subjects like sustainability, water use, energy, and life cycle analysis. Problem-sovling paradigms such as the Three E's (environment, economics, and engineering) and impact (population, consumption, and technology). Final project was a Monte Carlo simulation of the lake level and algae population of Lake Erie in MATLAB.

MSE 250

Materials Science and Engineering

Introductory-level materials science, mostly focused on metallurgy. Many hands-on labs including impact testing, annealing, and heat treatment. Microstructures, treatments, mechanical properties, diffusion, atomic bonds, phase diagrams, fatigue, and creep.


Activities
Leadership Advantage

A student-run four-day leadership seminar for incoming College of Engineering students taking place before the Fall semester begins. Fosters teamwork, professional development, and a welcoming environment. I was a participant in the program in 2020, and while the program was virtual due to COVID-19 campus restrictions, I learned many valuable tips for succeeding academically and professionally. I became a facilitator in 2021 as we returned to in-person instruction, leading a group of ten participants through the experience. Activities included an egg drop competition, lunches with corporate recruiters, and a trebuchet building competition. Since 2022, I have been on the Executive Board as the Secretary, managing communications with facilitators and participants.


Triangle Fraternity

A social fraternity of engineers, scientists, and architects. I pledged in the Spring of 2021 and have held leadership positions in every subsequent semester. As a member of the Judicial Board, I was to be knowledgeable of the constitution and bylaws of the organization and uphold the standards of our fraternity. As the community service chair, I organized outings to local parks to remove invasive species to help each member reach the required service hours per semester. As a member educator, I prepared and presented planned lessons to the 18-member pledge class about our history, ethics, etiquette, and expectations.


Society of Applied Engineering Sciences

A professional organization for the Applied Engineering Sciences major at Michigan State University. Network with other undergraduate students and corporate representatives from Fortune 500 companies for engineering and business roles. I joined shortly after I was admitted to the Applied Engineering Sciences program in the Fall of 2022, and I was one of the most active members that academic year. As of April 2023, I am on the Executive Board as the Director of External Relations; I organize service events to give back to the local community which supports us.


Students Consulting for Nonprofit Organizations

An entirely student-run organization offering pro-bono consulting services to charitable nonprofit organizations all over the country. During the Spring of 2023, I worked with a team of nine other students to provide services to a local crisis hotline. The primary goals were to increase reach on social media, connect with local resources, and advertise for long-term staff. I used Canva to create and distribute physical materials for advertising such as flyers and business cards.
